INFORMATION

The Venue

The Larmer Tree Gardens are 11 acres of ornate garden and beautiful surroundings set in the Dorset and Wiltshire countryside. You'll have plenty of time to explore the grounds, see the ponds and peacocks and bask in some early summer sun! We will be outside as much as we can, so feel free to bring rugs, sun-cream, panamas, and just in case, umbrellas, wellie boots!

Dress Code

Summer Garden Party

Think hipster, not church fete! Most of the day will be outside on the Lawn so dress appropriately. Heels are acceptable for the Ladies (or Men if that's your thing), but feel free to bring a change of shoes. We'll have some flip flops and heel stoppers just in case also!

Wedding Breakfast

You won't go hungry as we'll be serving a delicious feast for your table to forge on! It will satisfy all tastes, but please let us know if you have any special dietary requirements.

Getting To (And From!) The Larmer Tree

Driving

The postcode for the Gardens is SP5 5PY, however most sat navs will send you to Rushmore Golf Course, so be careful! The main approach roads are the A354 and A30, and from there you can follow the Brown Flower heritage signs. For more details, check the Larmer Tree website.

Taxis

The Gardens are on the Dorset/Wiltshire border, in the middle of nowhere! Please pre-book your taxi before the day, as it will not be possible to hail a cab in the evening.

Help a brother out! If you are looking for someone to share a cab with, or if you have space available then please let us know and we'll try to put people together.

Parking

There is a car park at the Gardens and it's free of charge. You are welcome to leave cars over night, but this is at your own risk and the Larmer Tree has told us to say that they are not responsible for your cars or belongings. Bore off.

Where To Stay

We can't hide it, The Larmer Tree is in the middle of nowhere, though there are a couple of B&B's nearby if you're lucky. Otherwise, the closest towns are not too far away - Wimborne, Shaftesbury and Blandford are all close and have a selection of places to stay - but dont forget to book a cab!

However, don't forget that it is a Bank Holiday weekend, and so if you want to make a weekend of it by the Sea, and join us for the wind down BBQ on the Sunday, then you might consider staying in Bournemouth so you will be centrally located. Again, plenty of choice there, but book up quick and don't forget the cab! (Please let us know your arrangements and we can try to help you with everything.)

You can also camp at the Larmer Tree, or stay there in your campervan if you have one, but please tell us in advance if you want to do this.

Google, AirBNB, OwnersDirect, Expedia, Booking.com, etc will all do a far better job than we can of recommending places, but if you feel a bit lost and want a bit of advice on what to do - please get in touch!
